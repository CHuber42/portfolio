Hobbies

My hobbies are constantly changing; throughout my life I have consistently enjoyed video games... however,
I find that they rarely provide me the fulfillment I have come to crave. They are not a hobby that is ever going to "take me anywhere"
therefore, more often than not, I wind up feeling as if I have burned my time into oblivion after having played them.

Outside of that, I have discovered two primary motivations for having a given hobby:

  1. That I am sharing an experience with someone (in which case, 'what we're doing' is mostly unimportant in the face of 'who I am doing it with')
  2. Building - or building towards - things. Examples are:
    • My metal arts, which I would love to have more time spend on (as well as a shop to do it in!)
    • Expanding my knowledge, with the goal of developing something ('doing science' falls in this category)
    • Bringing existing concepts in-line with their optimal functionality: think what Tesla has done with cars, applied to everything.
